MARY MITCHELL SLESSOR

LIFE : 2 December 1848 - 13 January 1915

More ---

About

  • Scotish presbyterian missionary
  • Christian missionary work in Africa
  • women's rights and rescuing children from infanticide

    • LIFE

    DATES MISSIONS
    1848 Born on 2nd of december in Gileomston, Aberdeen, Scotland
    1859 Worked as a half timer in a Baxter brothers mill where she spent half of her day at a school provided by the mill owners and other half working for the company.
    1862 Became a skilled jute worker, working from 6am to 6pm with just an hour for breakfast and lunch.
    1875 Decided to follow in the footsteps of david livingstone, the famous missionary and explorer when she heard he had died
    1876 She was trained in edinburg and set sail to west Africa on 5th of august 1876 where she was assigned to the calabar region in the land of the efik people. She was warned of the traditional beliefs and superstitions in relation to women giving birth to twins.
    1879 While trying to dive deeper into calabar, She contaracted malaria and was forced to return to Scotland to recover.
    1888 Travelled to an area in calabar were male missionaries had been killed and taught her teachings with the help of other missionaries and king Eyo Honesty II, Slessor was able to stop the killing of twins which was a particularly evil curse and adopted the children she found adopted.
    1915 Due to severe fevers from the malaria she had contacted, Slessor died on 13th of January 1915.
